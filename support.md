# DarkWalls — Support

DarkWalls is a quiet, curated wallpaper app for dark hours. This page answers the questions we get most often. If your question isn't here, send a short note to **privacy@orbontech.com** and we'll get back to you, usually within two business days.

---

## Getting started

### How do I save a wallpaper?

Open a wallpaper from the grid, then tap the **download** button in the bottom action bar. The first time you do this, iOS will ask for permission to add images to your Photos — tap **Allow Access**. Every saved wallpaper goes into a dedicated **DarkWalls** album inside Photos.

### How do I actually set it as my wallpaper?

DarkWalls saves the image to your photo library; iOS handles the rest. After saving:

1. Open the **Photos** app.
2. Find the image (in the **DarkWalls** album, or in your recents).
3. Tap the **Share** button (the square with the up-arrow).
4. Scroll down and tap **Use as Wallpaper**.

This is the standard iOS flow — no third-party app can set the wallpaper directly, which is by Apple's design.

### My saved wallpapers aren't in a "DarkWalls" album

On some iPhones — especially if you tapped **Selected Photos** instead of **Allow Access to All Photos** the first time — iOS saves images to the general library without creating the album. To fix:

1. Go to **Settings → Photos → DarkWalls**.
2. Grant **Full Access**.
3. Save one more wallpaper from inside the app. The album will appear.

## Favorites, downloads, and the catalogue

### Where are my favorites stored?

On your device, locally. We don't run servers that track which wallpapers you like — tapping a bookmark just records an ID in the app's private storage on your phone. Uninstalling DarkWalls permanently deletes your favorites.

### Can I back up my favorites?

Not through DarkWalls. This is a tradeoff we made for privacy — there's no account system, so there's no cloud backup either. If you switch phones, the built-in iOS backup (via iCloud or a direct Mac backup) will carry your favorites with the app. A fresh install on a new device with no phone backup starts empty.

### Why does the download button ask me to wait a few seconds?

DarkWalls is free. A short 5-second interstitial before the first few downloads helps keep the app independent. If you'd rather skip the pause entirely, you can tip the developer through **Settings → Support the Developer**. Any tip amount, through Ko-fi or PayPal, disables ad interruptions on your device.

### I tipped but ads are still showing

Open the app at least once after tipping so the "supporter" flag can be written. If it still hasn't kicked in, email **privacy@orbontech.com** with the platform you tipped through and we'll sort it out manually.

### How do I search for a specific mood or color?

Tap the search bar on the home screen or long-press the app icon on your home screen and pick **Search**. You can type a keyword (like "purple" or "forest") or tap one of the tag chips to filter. Selected tags combine — pick two to narrow further.

### The app is showing me the same wallpapers. Can I see more?

The catalogue is hand-curated, so it's intentionally small. We add new wallpapers periodically rather than flooding the library with generic images. Check **What's New** in the App Store for update notes.

## Other questions

### Is there an Android version?

Not at launch. DarkWalls is built on a cross-platform codebase, so an Android release is technically possible — if there's enough interest, we'll do the work. Let us know at **privacy@orbontech.com** if you'd like one.

### I'm a wallpaper artist — can I submit my work?

We curate very selectively, but we're happy to look at pitches. Send two or three samples (or links) to **privacy@orbontech.com** with a short note about your work. We reply to every submission, even if it's a no.

### The app crashed or behaved strangely

We don't have remote crash reporting (that would mean collecting device diagnostics, which we've opted not to do). So we rely on people like you telling us. Please write to **privacy@orbontech.com** with:

- Your iPhone model and iOS version (**Settings → General → About**)
- What you were doing when it went wrong
- A screenshot if you can get one

### What data does DarkWalls collect?

Effectively nothing that identifies you. No accounts, no tracking identifiers, no analytics. The only thing that leaves your device is an anonymous "this wallpaper was downloaded" ping so we can rank popularity — and it contains no information about you. For the full audit-level answer, read the [Privacy Policy](privacy-policy).

### How do I delete everything?

Uninstall the app. Every piece of locally-stored data (favorites, download counter, recent searches, supporter flag) is removed with the app. We have nothing on our servers to delete because we never had anything in the first place.
